Compare Your Options

Debt relief isn't one-size-fits-all.

Here's the honest tradeoff between the three most common paths, so you know what you're choosing before you talk to anyone.

Option A

Credit Counseling

A nonprofit agency negotiates lower interest rates; you repay the full balance over time.

  • Good if you can still afford minimums
  • Doesn't reduce principal owed
  • Smaller credit impact
Option B · Most requested

Debt Settlement

A company negotiates with creditors to settle for less than you owe, often over 24–48 months.

  • Best for larger unsecured balances
  • Can reduce total amount owed
  • Temporary credit score impact
Option C

Debt Consolidation Loan

One new loan pays off multiple debts, leaving a single fixed monthly payment.

  • Good if your credit still qualifies
  • Simplifies payments, not balance
  • Requires decent approval odds
